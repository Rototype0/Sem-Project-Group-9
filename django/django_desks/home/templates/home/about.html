Why stand?

Studies show prolonged sitting can lead to back pain, obesity, and cardiovascular diseases, while too much standing may cause fatigue and discomfort. Over time, neck strain the poor posture and back pain can lead to chronic musculoskeletal problems.

Furthermore, remaining sedentary for hours reduces circulation, increasing the risk of varicose veins and deep vein thrombosis (DVT). Studies also associate prolonged sitting with metabolic disorders, such as obesity and Type 2 diabetes, as well as a higher risk of cardiovascular diseases. A sedentary lifestyle can even impact mental well-being, contributing to fatigue, decreased focus, and stress.

However, studies also show that alternating between sitting and standing during work significantly improves health and productivity. By reducing the strain on the lower back and neck, standing desks help minimize the discomfort caused by prolonged sitting.

They encourage better posture, which can prevent long-term musculoskeletal issues. Increased movement throughout the day boosts blood flow, aiding concentration and reducing fatigue. Standing desks have also been linked to improved energy levels and reduced risk of weight gain, as standing burns more calories than sitting.

Additionally, the flexibility to adjust positions fosters an active work environment, enhancing overall workplace morale and efficiency.
